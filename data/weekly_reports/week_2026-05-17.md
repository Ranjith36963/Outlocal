### 3.3 Email Subject Line Analysis

**Winners (produced INTERESTED replies):**

| Pattern | Example | Replies |
|---|---|---|
| `[First name] — [town] [business type] flagged 'Not Secure'` | "Tom — your Ripon carpentry site isn't mobile-friendly" | 3/3 INTERESTED |
| `[Business name] — [specific issue]` | "Pennine Print & Design — [SSL issue]" | 1 INTERESTED (no-name fallback) |

**Confirmed subject-line rules (non-negotiable):**
1. **First name in subject if known** — outperforms business-name-only. Reduces spam-filter threshold.
2. **Town name always** — local credibility is a trust signal. Business owners respond to their own town.
3. **Specific, verifiable issue** — "flagged 'Not Secure'" beats "improve your presence". Specificity = credibility.
4. **No question marks** — statement of fact outperforms curiosity hook for trades/B2B cold email every time.
5. **Chrome's exact wording** — `'Not Secure'` (not "unsecured" or "insecure"). Credibility depends on matching what they see in their browser.

**Failing patterns (never use again):**
- Vague hooks ("Quick question about [Business Name]") — 0% INTERESTED from any variant
- Generic improvement framing ("help you get more customers online") — 0%
- Question marks in Day-0 subject — no data but confirmed anti-pattern by inference from non-results
