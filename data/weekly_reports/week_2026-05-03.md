### 3.3 Subject Line Patterns — What Worked vs. Failed

**Winners:**
| Pattern | Example | Result |
|---|---|---|
| `[First name] — [town] [business type] flagged 'Not Secure'` | "Tom — your Ripon carpentry site isn't mobile-friendly" | 3/3 INTERESTED |
| `[Business name] — [specific concrete issue]` | "Pennine Print & Design — [issue]" | 1 INTERESTED (fallback when no first name) |

**Confirmed rules:**
1. First name in subject outperforms business name only — personalisation reduces spam threshold
2. Town name always included — local credibility is a trust trigger
3. Specific and verifiable issue — "flagged 'Not Secure'" beats "improve your presence"
4. **No question marks in subject** — statement of fact outperforms curiosity hook for trades cold email
5. Chrome's exact wording (`'Not Secure'`) carries more weight than paraphrase

**Failing patterns (never use):**
- Vague hooks ("Quick question about [Business]")
- Generic improvement framing ("help you get more customers")
- Any question mark in a Day-0 subject line
